Historical Timeline

Slavery and Emancipation (1600s–1865)

The period of slavery had a huge impact on the early African American experience. Although millions of people were forced into slavery, their strength, culture, and determination helped shape future generations and set the stage for progress that would come later.

Reconstruction and Segregation (1865–1940s)

After emancipation, African Americans began to make important political and social gains, but segregation and discrimination still held many people back. These barriers shaped everyday life for generations to come.

Civil Rights Movement (1950s–1970s)

Leaders like Martin Luther King Jr., Rosa Parks, and many others brought people together through marches, protests, and boycotts to fight for justice and equal rights in America.

Modern Achievements (1980s–Today)

African Americans have continued to make major achievements in politics, the arts, sports, and technology, building on a long legacy of strength, creativity, and leadership.
